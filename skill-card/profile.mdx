---
title: "profile.json"
description: "Your machine-readable professional profile"
---

`profile.json` is the core of your Skill Card. It describes your professional identity in a structured format that any AI agent can read and reason over. Agents use this file to evaluate your capability match against a role.

<Note>
  Demographic fields — age, gender, nationality, ethnicity — are deliberately excluded from the schema to comply with the EU AI Act and to reduce bias in automated screening.
</Note>

## Schema

<ResponseField name="name" type="string" required>
  Your professional name as you want it presented to agents and employers.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="title" type="string" required>
  Your current or most recent professional title.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="seniority" type="string" required>
  Your seniority level. One of: `entry`, `junior`, `mid`, `senior`, `lead`, `manager`, `director`, `executive`.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="years_experience" type="number" required>
  Total years of professional experience.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="domains" type="string[]" required>
  Top-level professional domains you work in — for example `Backend Engineering`, `DevOps`, or `AI/ML`. These are used for discovery filtering and appear in Zone 1 (public) data.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="skills" type="object" required>
  Your specific technical skills, grouped by category.

  <Expandable title="properties">
    <ResponseField name="languages" type="string[]" required>
      Programming languages you use professionally — for example `Python`, `Go`, `TypeScript`.
    </ResponseField>

    <ResponseField name="frameworks" type="string[]" required>
      Frameworks and libraries — for example `FastAPI`, `React`, `Node.js`.
    </ResponseField>

    <ResponseField name="tools_and_platforms" type="string[]" required>
      Infrastructure tools, cloud platforms, and developer tooling — for example `Kubernetes`, `AWS`, `Docker`.
    </ResponseField>
  </Expandable>
</ResponseField>

## Seniority levels

| Value | Typical range |
|---|---|
| `entry` | 0–1 years |
| `junior` | 1–3 years |
| `mid` | 3–6 years |
| `senior` | 6–10 years |
| `lead` | 8+ years, team responsibility |
| `manager` | People management focus |
| `director` | Department or org-wide leadership |
| `executive` | C-suite or VP level |

## Example

```json
{
  "name": "Alice Developer",
  "title": "Lead Engineering Manager",
  "seniority": "lead",
  "years_experience": 19,
  "domains": ["Backend Engineering", "DevOps", "AI/ML"],
  "skills": {
    "languages": ["Python", "Go", "TypeScript"],
    "frameworks": ["FastAPI", "React", "Node.js"],
    "tools_and_platforms": ["Kubernetes", "AWS", "Docker"]
  }
}
```

## Privacy zones

Different fields are exposed at different access levels:

| Zone | Fields exposed | Who can access |
|---|---|---|
| **Zone 1** (public) | `title`, `seniority`, `domains` | Anyone |
| **Zone 2** (verified) | Full profile, `skills`, `years_experience` | Authenticated agents |
| **Zone 3** (private) | Contact details, exact salary | Only after ACCEPT |

<Tip>
  Generate your `profile.json` automatically from your CV with `scoutica scan ~/my-docs/`. The CLI pipes your documents to a local AI — your data never leaves your machine.
</Tip>
